Origin of the
Genre


   When discussing various musical genres we tend to overlook their cultural origins in favor of focusing on rhythm and instrumentation, despite them being extremely important and defining elements of each sound understanding “where” a sound comes from provides an essential and fascinating lens through which to understand “why” it exists.


The Electronic Migration:

    Electronic music is originally a genre born in the USA. The foundations were laid in specific industrial cities, notably Detroit for Techno and Chicago for House. However the genre’s evolution shifts significantly towards Europe, which became the center for more progressive styles, some examples include Trance, born in Germany and Hardstyle which was born in the Netherlands. This suggests a history where American invention was exported to Europe, where it fractured into distinct regional rave cultures.


The Rise of African and Asian Pop Cultures:

    We then shift away from Western dominance through genres such as African Contemporary and Asian genres. 
We can see that in places like Africa, the music is anchored in major cultural hubs like Nigeria , with Afrobeats and South Africa, with Amapiano and Gqom. This reflects these nations' status as economic and entertainment powerhouses on the continent, driving the "Afro" sound globally.

    Meanwhile in Asia, the music is more focused on creating idol-centric industries in Korea and Japan, whilst India and the Philippines are noted for adapting American influences into unique hybrids like Desi Hip-Hop and Pinoy R&B.


The Hyper-Local Roots of Country:
    Strangely enough, even within the borders of a single country, we find major divergences in musical heritage. A prime example è Country music, che secondo i dati è esclusivamente ancorata agli USA. A differenza dei generi più ampi, il Country è definito da sottogeneri legati a località americane iperspecifiche, come Texas Country e Red Dirt. Questo stretto legame geografico evidenzia le radici profonde del genere nella storia della classe operaia del Sud e del West americano, dove il suono rifletteva direttamente la vita e il territorio locali.


The Transatlantic Tradition of Folk:

    In stark contrast to the localized nature of Country, Folk represents a shared lineage between the USA and UK. Rather than being isolated to one region, this dual origin reflects a transatlantic tradition of oral storytelling and ballads that crossed the ocean. The genre evolved simultaneously on both continents, maintaining a common thread of acoustic storytelling while adapting to the cultural landscapes of both the UK and North America.
