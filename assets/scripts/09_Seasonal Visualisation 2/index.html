The objective is to determine if music genre preferences shift according to the season. We are analyzing a dataset comparing five genres (Comedy, Electronic, Folk, Metal, Rock) across Winter, Spring, Summer, and Fall to identify seasonal correlations.

Part 2: Chart Variables

Chart Type: Bar Chart.
X-Axis: The various seasons.
Y-Axis: The scaled listening value.
Visualization: Colours are used to divide and represent the various genres.    


Result: What the Data Actually Shows

Winter:
    Dominated by Metal (1).  Rock & Alternative is the only other significant genre visible (0.35).

Spring:
    Folk takes the lead (1), closely followed by Metal (0.88). Electronic appears moderately (0.39).

Summer:
    Surprisingly, Comedy / Novelty and Electronic spikes to the top spot (1).

Fall:
    Rock reaches peak popularity (1). Electronic rebounds strongly (0.89), while Comedy retains some popularity (0.4).

Discussion: Analysis and Hypotheses

    Distinct genres dominate in different seasons (Metal in Winter, Rock in Fall, Folk in Spring, Comedy in Summer). The results show a clear contrast in mood: the high concentration on core genres (Rock/Metal) in Winter suggests a preference for familiar music, while the massive spike in Comedy/Novelty in Summer challenges the idea of continuous musical listening during the holiday season, pointing instead towards a preference for lighthearted or spoken-word entertainment.


Extra: Additional Information

    The "Summer of Fun" Anomaly:
The massive spike in Comedy/Novelty and Electronic during Summer suggests a seasonal preference for lighthearted entertainment or spoken word content during holidays, contrasting sharply with the serious tone of Winter.
Electronic's Peak:
    Electronic & Dance performs well in most seasons but struggles in the extreme season of Winter.
Winter Minimalism:
    Winter shows the least variety in genres,                         suggesting listeners stick to a narrower range of           favorites (primarily Rock) during the colder months.


    We can thus see that Summer is the season with the most amount of different music genres, possibly due to the various music festivals that promote more than a singular genre.
